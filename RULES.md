# ⚔️ Napoleonic Tactics: Rules of Engagement

Welcome to **Napoleonic Tactics**, a turn-based strategy game of maneuver, supply, and attrition.

---

## 🚩 Objectives
To achieve total victory, you must accomplish **one** of the following:
1.  **Decapitation Strike**: Capture the enemy **Command Center (CC)** by moving any unit onto it.
2.  **Strategic Dominance**: Capture and hold the central **Capture Point** for **5 continuous turns**.
    *   *Note: If the enemy captures the point, your progress is reset to 0.*

---

## 📦 Supply & Reinforcements
Supply is the lifeblood of your army. It is used to call in reinforcements.
*   **Base Supply**: +100 per turn (if you control your Command Center).
*   **Supply Nodes**: +25 per turn for each node captured.
*   **Reinforcements**: Spend supply to spawn new units in empty tiles adjacent to your Command Center.
    *   *Note: Reinforcements cannot move or attack on the turn they arrive.*

---

## 💂 Regiment Types
Each unit has unique strengths and tactical roles:

| Unit Type | Role | Special Traits |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Line Infantry** | Main Battle Force | Fires in a straight line only. Can form **Box Formation**. |
| **Light Infantry** | Skirmishers | 360° firing arc. High evasion in Forest and Buildings. |
| **Cannon** | Artillery | Long range. Weak at close range. Can build **Palisades**. |
| **Hussars** | Light Cavalry | High mobility (5 MP). Massive bonus vs Light Infantry. |
| **Chasseurs** | Elite Skirmishers | Superior accuracy and 360° firing arc. |

---

## 🗺️ Terrain & Movement
Movement Points (MP) are consumed based on the terrain type:

| Terrain | MP Cost | Defense Bonus | Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Grass** | 1 | 0% | Standard terrain. |
| **Road** | 0.5 | 0% | Rapid movement. |
| **Forest** | 2 | +20% | High cover for Light Infantry. |
| **Buildings** | 2 | +30% | Excellent defensive position. |
| **Mountain** | 3 | +40% | Difficult to traverse. |
| **River** | 3 | -20% | Dangerous crossing; units are vulnerable. |

---

## ⚙️ Combat Mechanics
*   **Firing Lines**: Line Infantry can only shoot units directly in front of their facing (North, South, East, or West).
*   **Rotation**: You can rotate a unit's facing for **1 MP**. This is crucial for repositioning your firing line.
*   **Range**: Most units have a range of 2-3 tiles. Cannons can fire up to 5 tiles but deal minimal damage to adjacent targets.
*   **Damage**: Damage is calculated based on the number of men, attack stats, and the target's effective defense (terrain + entrenchment).

---

## 🛡️ Special Abilities
*   **Entrench (1 MP)**: Grants a +20% defense bonus. Removed if the unit moves.
*   **Box Formation (1 MP)**: (Line Infantry only)
    *   Grants 360° firing arc.
    *   Massive defense bonus against Cavalry.
    *   Reduces Attack and Max MP.
*   **Palisades (1 MP)**: (Cannons only)
    *   Grants a significant defense bonus.
    *   Restricts firing to a 90° frontal cone.

---

## 💡 Tactical Tips
*   **Protect the Flanks**: Line Infantry are vulnerable if the enemy moves outside their narrow firing line.
*   **Combined Arms**: Use Hussars to hunt down Light Infantry, and Cannons to soften up entrenched positions.
*   **Supply is Key**: Don't ignore the Supply Nodes in the center; they provide the extra resources needed for a prolonged war.
*   **The Center Holds**: The Capture Point is the fastest way to win if you can establish a strong defensive perimeter around it.
